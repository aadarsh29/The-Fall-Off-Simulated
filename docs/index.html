What You're Hearing

The stadium simulation adds convolution reverb tuned to a large arena, causing low frequencies to build up and linger. Transients — the sharp attacks of snares and hi-hats — become smeared and softened. The overall image widens and loses definition compared to the studio original.

What the Spectrogram Shows

In the spectrogram, this manifests as horizontal energy bleed around quiet sections, a denser and more elevated low-end band (0–3 kHz), and a more homogeneous mid-range wash. The stadium version never fully goes silent — the room keeps ringing.
